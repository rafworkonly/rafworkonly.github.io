title: Plane out, boat back
date: 2026-06-21

Sometimes, you have to be comfortable doing things by yourself. Over the past few years I've found myself doing quite a lot of solo activities and trips, never more than a day normally. It came originally from travelling with work, probably culminating in the 8 months I spent working in Cornwall. 

That could've been relatively isolating, I had a mate there as well, but he would normally head home at weekends. It also wasn't a super busy part of the world, so I had to basically sit in my room, or go out by myself. So, I did a lot of hiking, swimming, went for a surf regularly, and even a day trip to the Isles of Scilly (plane out, boat back). 

This has continued, especially the surfing and did do one night solo camping/surfing in Devon. I think there's some pressure, probably internally, to do things in groups, or maybe it appears awkward sometimes. But, having that thought limits you quite a lot, I wouldn't have done as much as I have if not for just becoming happy doing it myself. It also suits a spontaneous personality, a lot of my plans are quite last minute. 

I still love doing holidays, activities or just meeting with my mates, but getting comfortable doing things myself has let me do a lot. I mean, just the surfing is something I just wouldn't do if I hadn't had the drive to do it myself in Cornwall. 

I think a lot of people could do with trying it out, even if it's just some walking, it's pretty easy, and after the first couple times, any awkwardness just goes anyway.
