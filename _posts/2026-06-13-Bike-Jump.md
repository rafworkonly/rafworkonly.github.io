As a side hobby, I've spent the last couple months learning how to ride a mountain bike on a pump track. A pump track is a small concrete loop with a few jumps and turns, and the aim is to shift your bodyweight to propel yourself forward, rather than pedalling. 

Although the activity looks straightforward to a bystander, mastering skills on the pump track is far from easy. If you make a mistake, you will know quite quickly when you hit the floor. Sometimes I found myself approaching a corner and entering too high, slipping off the edge of the track, causing a calamitous but inconsequential fall. It feels like you're about to die, but somehow you remain unharmed. In this sense, the pump track will give you immediate and harsh feedback. Every obstacle on the pump track is real, there is no faking it. 

When you become good at a skill on the pump track, such as jumping, or doing a manual (lifting your front wheel off the floor), you will feel very connected to your bike, since it is required to perform the movement. That sense of one-ness with your bike also becomes available to you when you cycle to work, or go to the groceries. Bike maintenance becomes much easier, because you can identify problems with the bike by feel. 

Despite having to go around the track many times, I found there was a single step-like instance where I understood what I was supposed to be doing, that feeling was great. I highly recommend the activity.
