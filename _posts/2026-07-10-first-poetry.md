title: Capitalism takes the nothing from you
date: 2026-07-10

Like flossing our teeth with the thin stems of young
grass 
Wavering peacefully in the midday shade.
Like laying awake in the late hours of the morning listening out
for the whisper of the leaves
and the waving birdsongs
in the garden.
Taking the time to notice the wings of your
cigarette smoke
and the steam of my morning coffee
intertwine.
The nothing of having one flimsy ubmrella
in the pouring rain.
Of confronting dumb songs from obscure inside
jokes
out loud in the middle of the city.
The nothing of the gentle awareness
noticing the small cracks in the concrete.
The nothing of relaxing your gaze into the horizon
the future 
the past 
and the present 
alive in your mind.
The nothingness of stopping 
to pet a strangers dog.
The energy to strike up small conversations
with those that sit by you.
In the quiet way the colours shift with the 
rising sun
Or the joy of the baby rocked by its mother 
Or the stretching of each spring 
of the tendont 
in your legs as you lay on the grass.
The nothing
the silence 
the boredom
is consumed 
by our own minds
in the broken system.
