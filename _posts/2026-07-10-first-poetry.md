Like flossing our teeth with the thin stems of young
grass<br>
Wavering peacefully in the midday shade.<br>
Like laying awake in the late hours of the morning listening out<br>
for the whisper of the leaves<br>
and the waving birdsongs<br>
in the garden.<br>
Taking the time to notice the wings of your<br>
cigarette smoke<br>
and the steam of my morning coffee<br>
intertwine.<br>
The nothing of having one flimsy ubmrella<br>
in the pouring rain.<br>
Of confronting dumb songs from obscure inside<br>
jokes<br>
out loud in the middle of the city.<br>
The nothing of the gentle awareness<br>
noticing the small cracks in the concrete.<br>
The nothing of relaxing your gaze into the horizon<br>
the future<br>
the past<br>
and the present<br>
alive in your mind.<br>
The nothingness of stopping<br>
to pet a strangers dog.<br>
The energy to strike up small conversations<br>
with those that sit by you.<br>
In the quiet way the colours shift with the<br>
rising sun<br>
Or the joy of the baby rocked by its mother<br>
Or the stretching of each spring<br> 
of the tendont<br> 
in your legs as you lay on the grass.<br>
The nothing<br>
the silence<br>
the boredom<br>
is consumed<br>
by our own minds<br>
in the broken system.<br>
