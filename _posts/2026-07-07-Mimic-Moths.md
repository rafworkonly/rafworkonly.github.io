This morning this pretty fella was waiting at my bus stop Gifu, Japan.

Curious what it was, I pointed my phone camera and used Google Lens to search.

Google Lens confidently identified it as an ‘Epicopeia’ mimic moth, (comparison image below) overlooking the important fact that it was broad daylight and therefore not likely to be a nocturnal insect.
![Guide](/assets/images/moth.png)
If I were a lizard hunting in the forest, and I trusted AI to identify my insects, I would've eaten this toxic butterfly trusting that it was the tasty moth and then spent the rest of the day throwing up everywhere.

And I think that's something worth remembering.
